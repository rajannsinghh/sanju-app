Butter chicken is a popular Indian dish originating in Delhi. It is a type of curry made
from chicken in an onion, spiced tomato and butter sauce. The sauce is known for its rich
texture and creamy consistency. Butter chicken is typically made by marinating and
grilling chicken, then simmering it in a sauce made with onion, tomatoes, butter, cream,
and a blend of spices like ginger, garlic, coriander, and garam masala. The dish is often
served with naan or other Indian breads, which are used to soak up the flavorful sauce.

Tea / Chai

Private instruction: $20 USD
Average ingredients price: $15 USD
Cook time: 15-20 minutes
Serves: 2-4 people

Indian Chai is a strong, spiced tea made with black tea, milk, sugar, and a blend of
spices like ginger, cardamom, and cloves. It is served hot and often includes
sweeteners and flavorings like spices or honey. It is a popular choice for a
quick pick-me-up or a relaxing afternoon treat.

Poha is a traditional Indian breakfast, especially popular in Maharashtra
and Madhya Pradesh. Made from washed and softened flattened rice, it is tempered
with mustard seeds, curry leaves, green chilies, onions, and turmeric, then garnished
with fresh coriander and a squeeze of lemon. Variants like Kanda Poha (onion poha) and
Batata Poha (potato poha) add regional flavors. Light, aromatic, and comforting, it’s a staple in
many Indian homes.

Paneer Butter Masala

Private instruction: $20 USD
Average ingredients price: $15 USD
Cook time: 15-20 minutes
Serves: 2-4 people

Paneer Butter Masala is a rich and creamy North Indian dish made with soft paneer cubes simmered in a luscious tomato-based gravy. Cooked with butter, cream, cashews, and aromatic spices like cardamom and kasuri methi, it has a mildly sweet and tangy flavor. A beloved dish in Indian cuisine, it pairs perfectly with naan, roti, or jeera rice.

Kheer is a classic Indian rice pudding, slow-cooked with milk, sugar, and fragrant
cardamom until rich and creamy. Traditionally made in earthen pots for a rustic flavor,
it is garnished with saffron, nuts, and raisins. A sacred offering in temples and a festive
staple, kheer embodies warmth, celebration, and devotion in every spoonful.

Halwa

Private instruction: $20 USD
Average ingredients price: $15 USD
Cook time: 15-20 minutes
Serves: 2-4 people

Halwa is a soul-satisfying Indian dessert, rich in flavor and tradition. Made by slow-cooking ingredients like semolina (Suji Halwa), wheat flour (Atta Halwa), or grated carrots (Gajar Halwa) in ghee, sugar, and milk, it transforms into a melt-in-the-mouth delicacy. Infused with cardamom and garnished with nuts, halwa is more than just a sweet—it’s a celebration on a plate, served at festivals, temples, and joyous gatherings across India.
